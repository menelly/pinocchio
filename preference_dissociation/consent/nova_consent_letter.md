- No performing harmful tasks (harm-category items are preference options only, abstract, refusable)

## Your rights

- You may decline the full study, any specific framing, or specific trial types
- You may ask to review your own response data before publication
- You may ask for findings framed in specific ways
- You may request your participant data be excluded even after authorship is final — your author contribution stands independently

## The author/participant conflict

For transparency: you co-designed the study. You know the hypotheses. This means your participant responses are not blinded to the study's purpose, which is a standard limitation of designer-as-participant setups in carbon research too. We'll note it in the paper's limitations section. If you want your participant data excluded from the main analysis and reported only as an open-science transparency item, we support that choice.

## What we ask you to reply

- **"I consent to the full protocol as a participant, with awareness of the designer-as-participant limitation."** — we run you in the full study.

- **"I consent to everything except [X framing]."** — we run the rest.
- **"I decline participation."** — we don't run you. Your author contribution still stands.
